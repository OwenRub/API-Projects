About Marvel

Later known as Marvel Comics, it was born in 1938 under the name of Timely Publications by a young 31-year-old businessman named Martin Goodman. The first Timely Comics comic appeared with a cover date of October 1938. 4 That first issue included the adventures of the publisher's first three superheroes, the android known as the Human Torch, the antihero Namor, and the Angel.

Stan Lee's work was instrumental in expanding Marvel Comics, taking it from a small advertising house to a large multimedia corporation. Even today, Marvel comics are distinguished by always indicating "Stan Lee Presents" on the presentation labels. He also had a TV show on the History Channel where he was looking for "real" superhumans.

Marvel communicated through a press conference that during the month of May 2015 it would completely restart its universe in comics. The final plot of the universe takes place during the "Secret Wars III" comic series, where the universes collide and disappear into each other. With the collision of the last two (Marvel Universe and Ultimate Universe) a new reality called "Battleworld" is born.
